The FortyTwo Code of Conduct

Part 1: Respect The Code, Respect Our Users

“Protect me from knowing what I don’t need to know. Protect me from even knowing that there are things to know that I don’t know. Protect me from knowing that I decided not to know about the things that I decided not to know about. Amen.”
― Douglas Adams, The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy

Kifi is a powerful tool that gives great value to many users in their daily habits. Our users use it for everything, from work to personal uses, from browsing to communicating with others. To provide this value, our technology and products have great powers, and with this powers comes great responsibility.

Our users are trusting us with their most private information. We expect our team at FortyTwo to be respectful not only to each other, but also to our users. In order to provide the great value of Kifi to our users we are storing information related to our users on our servers. Some information is being provided to us explicitly by our users, and some we collect is based on users’ behavior while using Kifi.

Since it is not only our legal but also our moral obligation towards our users to make sure that their private information is safe and protected, we require all of our employees to understand this code of conduct. This code of conduct is meant to guide FortyTwo employees with the proper rules of conduct that are required when having access to users’ private information.

In order to maintain our integrity toward our users and make Kifi not only an awesome product, but also a trustworthy one, please read, agree and sign this code of conduct in order to make sure that Kifi is not only the best product for our users, but also that all of us are obligated to principles of trust, privacy and integrity.

So “Don’t Panic,” just read the code, understand it, and implement its principles in your day to day.

Part 2: Keeping Private Information Safe

FortyTwo’s reputation is not only measured by how good is our product, but is also based on the way we protect our users personal and private data stored on our servers. In order to maintain our user’s trust, all employees should follow the 4.2 rules of privacy:

  1. Know and Follow Kifi’s Privacy Policy
    Our public Privacy Policy (published on kifi.com/privacy) is a binding declaration between us and our users. We believe that we should collect only the information necessary to provide the great value Kifi. All employees must read, know and respect Kifi’s Privacy Policy.

  2. Speak Up
    ​If you think that you are, or any of your colleagues are not following this Code of Conduct or FortyTwo’s Privacy Policy, please don’t be quiet - speak, report and prevent any behaviour that might compromise Kifi’s integrity.

  3. Limit Your Access to Data
    Our users are keeping different pages that they find interesting and important. They are also convesing using Kifi on private issues. Looking and searching on a user’s kifi data history can tell us a lot about that user, but sometimes it can tell us a lot more than they intended for us to know. Therefore, even when you are needed to access a specific Kifi user’s data, please respect our users’ privacy and do not access any data that is not necessary for your work. To be clear, you may not query or access a specific user’s data unless it’s:

    1. To technically investigate or fix a problem related to a suspected malfunction with Kifi’s functionality.
    2. To provide support for this user when the user reports a problem or a question.
    3. To understand behavior when planning or building a feature, not targeting a specific user’s personal information.

  4. Mum’s the Word

    1. We do not share or disclose data, aggregated data or any other user information with anyone outside of FortyTwo. Remember, even though data is collected, it doesn’t mean that it can be shared.
    2. If you think that disclosure of information is required for any reason, consult with with the company’s leadership (Currently: CEO or CTO) on whether this information should and can be disclosed. Any disclosure of information can only be done after receiving a written permission.

Part 3: Help us improve

If you have any doubts on whether your actions might breach the Code of Conduct, feel free to review the Code of Conduct and Privacy Policy and don’t hesitate to ask questions. We want you not only to read the code, but to actually understand it and help us improve it.

We expect all FortyTwo employees to not only read the code, but to follow and implement its principles. Breaching the Code of Conduct can not only harm our users, but can also harm Kifi’s trustworthiness and in some cases breach the law.
