	<h1 class="story-header">MtGox bitcoin exchange files for bankruptcy</h1>

     
         
 
    
    
		
        <div class="caption full-width">
  <img src="http://news.bbcimg.co.uk/media/images/73276000/jpg/_73276427_021307557-1.jpg" width="512" height="288" alt="Bitcoin trader Kolin Burges" />

    <span style="width:512px;">MTGox&#039;s shut down prompted some bitcoin traders to picket the exchange&#039;s offices</span>
  </div>
	<div class="story-feature related narrow">
		<a class="hidden" href="#story_continues_1">Continue reading the main story</a>		<h2>Related Stories</h2>
		<ul class="related-links-list">
						




	


	




<li>
	<a class="story" rel="published-1393422359347" href="/news/technology-26352442">Troubled MtGox Bitcoin boss emerges</a>

		</li>


											




		


	




<li class="has-icon-boxedwatch ">
	<a class="story" rel="published-1393362680641" href="/news/uk-26346087">Bitcoin: MtGox exchange goes offline<span class="gvl3-icon gvl3-icon-boxedwatch"> Watch</span></a>

		</li>


											




	


	




<li>
	<a class="story" rel="published-1393231823001" href="/news/technology-26319247">MtGox chief quits Bitcoin Foundation</a>

		</li>


											</ul>
	</div>
                      <p class="introduction" id="story_continues_1">The MtGox bitcoin exchange has filed for bankruptcy protection, reports say.</p>
        <p>The application was made in Japan by lawyers acting on behalf of the exchange and comes only days after MtGox went offline.</p>
        <p>On Tuesday, the exchange&#039;s boss said he was working hard to find a &quot;solution to our recent issues&quot;.</p>
        <p>Before going offline, technical troubles meant it prevented customers transferring digital cash to other exchanges on 7 February.</p>
        <p>Details of the bankruptcy are scant but the application for protection has been accepted by a district court in Tokyo, reported AFP. At the court hearing, the company said it had outstanding debts of about 6.5bn yen (£38m).</p>
        <p>MtGox&#039;s lawyers are believed to have decided to apply to the court for protection after US regulators filed a subpoena against the company.</p>
        <p>Reports suggested the site shut down after it discovered that an estimated 744,000 bitcoins - about $350m (£210m) - had been stolen due to a loophole in its security.</p>
        <p>MtGox&#039;s troubles have put pressure on the price bitcoin owners can get for their holdings. Currently one bitcoin is worth about $561 (£334), a price far lower than the high of $1,000 per coin it hit in November 2013.</p>
        <p>Meanwhile, Vietnam has banned its banks from from handling the crypto-currency saying the virtual cash is not legal tender. The country&#039;s state bank said trading in bitcoins carried &quot;potential risks&quot; for users.</p>
        <p>At the same time, Japan&#039;s deputy finance minister said any regulation of bitcoin would have to involve international cooperation to avoid opening up loopholes that traders could exploit.</p>
